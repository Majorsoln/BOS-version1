## Mission Question

Every concept document this Term produces, every primitive it defines, and every invariant it enforces must answer one question:

> **"How do we build a kernel that holds under decades of pressure — new verticals, new countries, AI that must remain advisory — and still proves to a court that nothing was ever silently changed?"**

The question has two halves, and the Kernel must satisfy both simultaneously. The first half — *holds under decades of pressure* — demands that the Kernel absorb change without a full rewrite: new engines, new compliance regimes, new AI models, none of which exist today. The second half — *proves to a court* — demands that every event, every document, and every audit trail remain intact, verifiable, and legally defensible no matter how much the system grows around them. A Kernel that flexes but cannot prove its integrity is useless. A Kernel that proves its integrity but cannot flex is disposable.
