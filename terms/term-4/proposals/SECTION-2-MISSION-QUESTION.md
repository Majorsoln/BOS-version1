# Term 4 — Foundation · Section 2: Mission Question

## 2. Mission Question

> How do we make a foundation neutral enough to know no vertical and no country, yet durable enough to carry all of them, and provable enough to survive a court audit?

This is THE question for Foundation because it forces the three demands the Kernel must satisfy into a single sentence and refuses to let any one of them be answered alone. Neutrality by restraint keeps the Kernel from naming a vertical, a country, or an integration; durability keeps it carrying every one of them across decades without a periodic rewrite; provability keeps every recorded fact reconstructable and defensible all the way up to a court audit. None is optional, and the Kernel is judged on holding all three at once.

What makes it the right organising question is that these three pulls are in tension. Neutrality resists the concrete commitments that durability seems to want; provability demands rigid, append-only history while durability demands room to absorb the unforeseen. A weaker question would optimise one pull and quietly sacrifice the others. By stating all three together, this question makes every Foundation decision answer to the same standard — and turns that tension into the design constraint the Kernel is built to resolve, rather than a problem to be discovered later.
